Unified Solution of Remote Access is an European LDV project of transfer of the innovative study technologies, for remote access laborarories and experiments on Embedded & Electronics Systems and Robotics.

The project aims to contribute to continuous education of professional workers in a broad field of engineering domains, raising the competences of new and practising engineers through contemporary learning tools and methods and place the European technology oriented SME companies in a better position for global market competition.

about

USORA - Unified Solution of Remote Access, is an European Comission - Lifelong Learning Programme funded project, that aims to transfer the innovative study technology concept of the DistanceLab and VirtualLab platforms (from Estonia and Germany to Portugal), for remote and virtual lab experiments. DistanceLab system will also be integrated with a novel robotic platform developed in Portugal, called Magabot.

Our goal is to contribute to raise levels of understanding of Integrated Circuit Technology and embedded systems for Vocational Education and Training students. We aim to provide a platform for continuous education of professional workers in a broad field of engineering domains The outcomes of this project involve raising the competence of new and practicing engineers through contemporary learning tools and methods and place European technology oriented SME companies in a better position for global market competition.

technology

Remote Lab

Remote Lab Centres are specific location-based labs which can be accessed over the Internet. A Remote Lab Center focuses on specific equipment or a series of experiments by giving online access, enabling control and monitoring of the process. Remote Lab Centres are located in different schools and offer through a resource sharing concept, different remote labs, such as; mobile robot lab, microcontroller testbed, automotive lab and smart house lab.

    Remote Lab Centres will be set up in following locations:
  • Tallinn, Estonia
  • Võru, Estonia
  • Helsinki, Finland
  • Bochum, Germany
  • Almada, Portugal

Virtual Lab

The existing Virtual Lab solution is composed by a web Java-based app which provides, emulation, virtualization and simulation of HomeLab Remote Kit embedded system and a robotic manipulator, besides the traditional tasks of editing, compiling, uploading and running code on the virtual target system.

Magabot

As it concerns the integration of the robotic platform Magabot in the Remote Lab web, the consortium is still studying the best technology to integrate the robot, but we’ve come at now with the following solution:

  • Magabot will be equipped with laptop running ROS (Robot Operating System);
  • The laptop that Magabot carries on-board will be connected to the web-server which will be running another ROS instance;
  • The web server will provide the web platform for the final user to interface with the robot, as well as it'll provide one or more video streams of the robot.
